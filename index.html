About Me

I'm M Srinivasan, currently a Visitor Research Intern at the TrustWare Research Group in SUTD Singapore under Prof. Dr Ezekiel and Sarang Nambiar. My research focuses on LLM security, safety, and robustness, particularly exploring vulnerabilities such as backdoor attacks and building resilient AI systems.

Prior to this, I worked at IBM Research Labs India with the AI for Quantum Data Engineering Team. There, I collaborated with leading research engineers such as Nigel Jones, Karla Spuldaro, Darin Baumgartel and Serjik Dikaleh, where I helped design and implement GraphRAG systems for intelligent data catalogs, enabling data engineering teams to efficiently query and explore metadata.

My work bridges AI research and practical engineering, combining cutting-edge machine learning techniques with real-world applications. I am deeply interested in ML system robustness, security in large language models, and graph-based retrieval systems. Beyond research, I enjoy hiking, photography, and travelling.

Adversarial Robustness of Deep Learning Models for Inland Water Body Segmentation from SAR Images

Siddharth Kothari, Srinivasan M, Sankalp Kothari, Jaya Sreevalsan Nair and Ujjwal Verma

📍 Submitted to JSTARS - IEEE Journal of Selected Topics in Applied Earth Observations and Remote Sensing

We study inland water body segmentation in SAR images using U-Net, focusing on the impact of noisy manual annotations. By simulating annotation errors as adversarial attacks, we show U-Net tolerates limited corruption before performance degrades. This highlights the critical role of annotation quality in segmentation tasks.

Addressing Modality Mismatch in Real Estate Recommendation Using Image Captioning

Srinivasan M, Siddharth Kothari, Munagala Kalyan Ram, Sankalp Kothari, Raghuram Bharadwaj

📍 Accepted at FMLDS 2024, Sydney Australia - 2024 IEEE International Conference on Future Machine Learning and Data Science (FMLDS)

We study modality mismatch in real estate recommendation using image captioning. Our approach leverages advanced NLP techniques to align visual and textual information, improving recommendation accuracy. We evaluate our method on a large dataset, demonstrating significant gains over baseline models.

Guitar-For-All-A Programmable Single Touch Synthetic Guitar

R Nitheezkant, Barath S Narayan, Madhav Rao, Ketaki Srikrishna Tamhanakar, Neha Srikrishna Tamhanakar, Srinivasan M

📍 Accepted at GEM 2023, Bridgetown Barbados - 2023 IEEE Gaming, Entertainment, and Media Conference (GEM)

We present a synthetic guitar that replaces complex fretting with single-button chord input, improving accessibility for beginners, therapeutic use, and individuals with disabilities. Using a microcontroller and MIDI synthesizers, the system maps strings to notes and generates chords in real time. Evaluation confirms accurate note/chord generation and responsive timing for practical use.
